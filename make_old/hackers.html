tools and materials

See the list below for what we're providing you to hack with. We suggest all hackers bring a laptop. Other than that, leave it up to us! If you'd like to see something not on the list below, shoot us an email at team@makemit.org. We might be able to provide it, otherwise you're free to bring something off-the-shelf as long as it is pre-approved. Please note that any other materials will be confiscated at registration.

featured materials

Formlabs 3D Printers

Want to build something but didn't think it was possible? Formlabs is bringing a fleet of their newest Form 1 STL printers for you to print with. Come use one of our nifty 3D printers to create a part of crazy geometry or of immense complexity!

MakerBot Replicator 2

MakerBot 3D printers are making an appearance at MakeMIT. Used everywhere by designers and engineers, the Makerbot Replicator 2 printers let you build things better and faster. These Desktop FDM 3D printers allow you to make prototypes, products, parts, inventions, tools, toys, and more.

Microsoft Kinects

Thanks to the Microsoft Kinect teams, both V1 AND the UNRELEASED V2 Kinects are available to fulfill your vision needs. If you decide to use the V2, we'll lend you a Microsoft Surface with the unreleased SDK. Not familiar with the Kinect system? Not a problem! There will be a team of Kinect engineers on-site to help guide your project along.

arLCDs

EarthLCD's newest product, arLCD, allows you to add a touchscreen interface to your Arduino project in minutes, rather than hours or days. Make a Custom LCD project with EarthLCD. It has never been easier to create a custom project. We're expecting to see arLCD at the heart of many cool project at this year's MakeMIT!

GrabCAD

GrabCAD makes it easy for your team to collaborate on all your CAD files. GrabCAD has offered to give anyone at MakeMIT who uses it a free year long subscription! It can sync up to Solidworks, Autodesk, and other modeling applications witih your Toolbox. Stop by the GrabCAD booth at MakeMIT to find out more and set it up on your computer!

Cyclone Linear Actuator

Rise Robotics enables low-cost mobile robotic arms that lift with human-speed and human strength. Cyclones have power at prices similar to pneumatic cylinders. Unlike existing servos or cylinders, Cyclones are lightweight, affordable, and powerful. They are battery powered and digitally controlled.
